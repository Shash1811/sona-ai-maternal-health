# WHO Infant Feeding & Complementary Nutrition Guidelines

## Official WHO Sources

- **Nutritional Management of Infant Complementary Feeding**
  - **Direct PDF Link:** [Download Guidelines (PDF)](https://platform.who.int/docs/default-source/mca-documents/policy-documents/operational-guidance/are-ch-38-03-operationalguidance-2018-eng-cpg-complementary-feeding-protocol.pdf)
  - **Summary:** Explicit rules on infant feeding. WHO mandates exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months. Between 4 to 6 months, babies may show readiness for solid food. It details the exact meal frequencies (e.g., 2-3 times per day at 6-8 months) and proper food consistencies (pureed to finger foods).

- **WHO Infant and Young Child Feeding Fact Sheet**
  - **Resource Link:** [WHO Feeding Fact Sheet](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/infant-and-young-child-feeding)
  - **Summary:** Outlines the core principles of responsive feeding, safe preparation of foods, and the necessity of nutrient-dense complementary foods starting at 6 months alongside continued breastfeeding up to 2 years or beyond.
